## Onboarding Flow Summary

**Estimated completion time:** 3–5 minutes
**Quiz screens:** ~22 questions + ~7 interstitials/loading screens
**Email gate position:** Screen 32 (after ~29 quiz screens — earlier than Buddhism's screen 41)

### Stage 1 — Hook (1 screen)
"Replace scrolling with Philosophy." Same anti-phone-addiction hook as the Buddhism variant, with a classical philosopher-figures illustration (Socrates/Plato style) instead of a Buddha. "Take our 2-minute quiz to get started." The topic word is styled in green italic — the only colour on an otherwise monochrome screen, drawing the eye directly to "Philosophy."

### Stage 2 — Demographics (2 screens)
Age selection only. Same as Buddhism — quick, low-friction.

### Stage 3 — Goals & early social proof (2 screens)
"Why are you interested in learning Philosophy?" Multi-select with emoji icons: Broaden my knowledge, Self-improvement, Build a learning habit, Feed my curiosity, Replace social media. Then immediately "Over 5 million people are already learning Philosophy with us." — social proof injected right after the first engagement action.

### Stage 4 — Validation interstitial (1 screen)
"We're here to help you achieve your goals!" with a Learn → Remember → Apply diagram. Same framework as Buddhism. Warms the user and reframes the product as a system, not just content.

### Stage 5 — Period selection (1 screen)
"Which periods of Philosophy interest you most?" — a Philosophy-specific multi-select. Options include time periods (Ancient, Medieval, Modern, Contemporary) or schools. This is unique to the Philosophy variant; Buddhism uses aspects/themes instead of historical periods.

### Stage 6 — Topic swiping (2 screens)
Yes/No illustrated cards for individual philosophy topics (Ethics and Morality with scales-of-justice illustration, plus at least one more topic). No Continue button — instant tap and advance. Shorter than Buddhism's 3-card topic section.

### Stage 7 — Expert credibility interstitial (1 screen)
"Crafted by philosophy experts" — same pattern as Buddhism's "Created by Buddhism experts." Trust signal before knowledge questions begin.

### Stage 8 — Knowledge self-assessment (1 screen)
"Are you satisfied with your knowledge of Philosophy?" — 👍 Yes / 🤚 A little / 👎 No. Same format as Buddhism; auto-advance.

### Stage 9 — Skills & knowledge mini-quiz (4 screens)
Multi-select skills question, then two knowledge quiz questions using famous philosophical thought experiments:
- "Do Stoics ignore what they can't control?" (Yes/No with Stoicism framing)
- "Would a utilitarian divert the trolley to save more lives?" (Yes/No with trolley-problem illustration)

These are more intellectually provocative than Buddhism's knowledge questions — they use classic philosophical dilemmas rather than factual recall. Followed by interstitial: "Here's something most people don't realize."

### Stage 10 — Learning behaviour (2 screens)
Time availability (Yes/Occasionally/No), biggest obstacles (multi-select). Condensed compared to Buddhism — the philosophy variant omits the "Philosophy made simple" product-pitch interstitial shown as a separate screen in Buddhism's equivalent stage.

### Stage 11 — Second knowledge round (3 screens)
Two more philosophy knowledge questions using famous quotes and allegories:
- "Who said 'I think, therefore I am'?" (Descartes — multiple choice with illustration)
- "What did the Allegory of the Cave explain?" (Plato — multiple choice with illustration)

Then "Backed by science. Designed to be fun and effective." credibility screen. These multiple-choice questions have 3–4 options and are more quiz-like than the Yes/No format used earlier.

### Stage 12 — Learning style & motivation (3 screens)
"When learning I connect best with:" (Big-picture ideas / Step-by-step details / Hands-on practice / Real-world examples / Stories and narratives), then "What's motivating you towards personal growth?", then "Join over 5 million people choosing lifelong learning" social proof close.

### Stage 13 — Plan builder loading (3 screens)
"Crafting your learning experience..." — animated progress bar labelled "Setting goals" with a 5-star testimonial below. Followed by daily learning goal selection UI (appears to offer time-per-day options). Shorter than Buddhism's 4-screen loading section.

### Stage 14 — Account creation gate (2 screens)
"Achieve your goals with Imprint — Create your free account." Email + password form. Google App of the Year, Apple Editors' Choice, Apple Essential Education award badges below the form. Same gate design as Buddhism, but positioned ~9 screens earlier in the flow.

### Stage 15 — Growth plan reveal (1 screen)
"Your Growth Plan" — personalised plan summary shown immediately after account creation. This is a brief screen before the offer.

### Stage 16 — Offer & paywall (3 screens)
1. **Tagline:** "Become the most interesting person in the room" — benefit framing
2. **Risk reversal:** "Try Imprint, risk-free." — dedicated full screen for 30-day money-back guarantee badge
3. **Payment:** Stripe credit card form (plan selection may have appeared between screens, not captured)

## Philosophy vs Buddhism — Variant Differences

| Dimension | Philosophy | Buddhism |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Total screens | 39 | 53 |
| Email gate position | Screen 32 | Screen 41 |
| Topic-swipe cards | 2 | 3 |
| Knowledge questions | 4 (2× Yes/No dilemmas + 2× multi-choice) | 5 (Yes/No recall) |
| Knowledge question style | Famous dilemmas & quotes (trolley problem, Descartes, Plato) | Factual recall (Middle Way, Dhammapada, Four Noble Truths) |
| Historical periods question | Yes (unique to Philosophy) | No |
| Plan chooser screen | Not captured (may be in payment flow) | Yes (3 plans with countdown timer) |
| Loading screen count | 3 | 4 |

The Philosophy variant is ~26% shorter than Buddhism. The earlier email gate (screen 32 vs 41) represents a significantly more aggressive collection strategy. The knowledge question style differs meaningfully: Buddhism uses factual recall questions, Philosophy uses famous dilemmas and quotes — which are more engaging for non-practitioners and create stronger "I want to know the answer" curiosity loops.

## Ideas to Test for Fabulous Web Onboarding

1. **Earlier email gate** — Philosophy collects email at screen 32 (~82% through the quiz), vs Buddhism at screen 41 (~77%). Both are well before the paywall. Test moving Fabulous's email gate to ~75–80% of quiz completion; the Philosophy data suggests this is viable without killing completion.

2. **Curiosity-gap trivia questions** — The trolley problem and Descartes/Plato questions are famous enough that users feel a pull to answer even without prior knowledge. For Fabulous, questions like "What's the #1 habit that keeps people feeling productive?" or "Do morning rituals actually change outcomes?" could create the same pull.

3. **Historical/framework selector** — Philosophy's period selector ("Ancient / Modern / Contemporary") gives users a sense of academic depth and personalisation before the quiz. A Fabulous equivalent: "Which area of habit science interests you most?" (Behaviour change / Sleep optimisation / Mindfulness / Productivity).

4. **"Become the most interesting person in the room" benefit framing** — Intellectual aspiration as a motivator. Fabulous users often have social/status goals alongside self-improvement. A screen framing Fabulous as making you "the person everyone asks for life advice" could resonate.

5. **Topic-swipe section (auto-advance Yes/No cards)** — Confirmed across both Imprint variants as the highest-momentum section. Worth testing a short 3–5 screen swipe section on Fabulous (e.g., "Does this habit interest you?" with illustrated cards for Sleep / Focus / Movement / Calm / Nutrition).

6. **"Your Growth Plan" reveal post-email** — Imprint shows a personalised plan screen immediately after account creation, before the offer. This reinforces the value of registering and primes the user to receive the plan as theirs before pricing appears. Test a similar "Here's your Fabulous plan" screen between email capture and payment.

7. **Dedicated risk reversal screen** — Both Imprint variants use a full screen for the 30-day guarantee, before any pricing. Separating guarantee from price screen reduces the cognitive load at the most anxiety-prone moment.

8. **Award badges on the email gate** — Confirmed in both variants. Test Fabulous's press/award logos (if available) on the email capture step.
