## Onboarding Flow Summary

This variant is a **direct-to-quiz** entry — no homepage, no brand introduction. The URL lands immediately on the gender + nervous system question. Total estimated completion time: **8–12 minutes** (39 questions is genuinely long, but the Likert format moves fast).

### Stage 1 — Hook & Diagnostic Entry (screens 01–02)
Opens on "Is your nervous system dysregulated?" — a loaded clinical question the user doesn't know the answer to, creating immediate curiosity. The subtitle "break free from survival mode" mirrors real felt experience. CTA badge reads "Take 3-minute quiz" (a claim, not reality — quiz is ~10 min). Gender and age collected first; age screen includes reassurance copy: "We only use your age to personalize your plan."

### Stage 2 — Psychological Safety Setup (screen 03)
"We're glad you are here" welcome with hands/connection photo and "honest reflection" framing. Sets a non-judgmental tone before sensitive questions. Counter shows "1/39" — manages expectations explicitly.

### Stage 3 — Problem Amplification: Likert Battery (screens 05–34, Q1–Q30)
30 emoji-scale statements covering the full spectrum of nervous system dysregulation symptoms: physical tension, sleep issues, emotional numbness, social withdrawal, libido drop, brain fog, burnout, hypervigilance, and more. The format is fast (one tap per screen) and emotionally accumulative — each "strongly agree" deepens the user's self-diagnosis.

### Stage 4 — Trauma & Context Questions (screens 35–43, Q31–Q38)
Shifts from Likert to open multi-choice: childhood emotional stress, current life stressors, mental symptoms (overthinking, anxiety, burnout), physical symptoms, areas to improve, desired emotional states, and CBT knowledge. This builds a rich personal profile while escalating emotional investment.

### Stage 5 — Social Proof & Credibility (screens 44–45)
Q39: "Did you hear about Liven from a therapist?" — a subtle credibility signal: implying therapists recommend Liven. Immediately followed by a social proof wall ("Join over 2,500,000 people") with face avatars and reaction snippets.

### Stage 6 — Email Gate (screens 46–48)
"Enter your email to see your personal Profile Summary" — frames email as a prerequisite to accessing results. Privacy reassurance below the field. Then an email marketing opt-in with asymmetric framing: "Sure, I'm in!" vs faded "I don't want to receive tips or updates."

### Stage 7 — Name + Results Reveal (screens 49–51)
Name collected before the results reveal. Results screen shows face photo + nervous system gauge + 4 named metrics (Functional Freeze, Systemic Overload, etc.) — high personal resonance, looks scientific.

### Stage 8 — Plan Building + Commitment (screens 52–54) ⭐ NEW
**New vs March 20:** A calendar timeline screen appears before loading: "A plan designed for women 25-34 to support your wellbeing journey — we expect you to decrease your nervous system dysregulation level by June 2026." Shows April → June months with app screenshot thumbnails. This sets a concrete future expectation (specific date) and previews the product. Loading screen uses Trustpilot ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ reviews while plan is "created."

### Stage 9 — Pre-Paywall: Progress Chart (screens 55–57) ⭐ CHANGED
**Different from March 20:** Instead of the "Scratch & Save" gamified discount screen, this variant shows an animated progress chart — "Your nervous system's safety level" with a rising curve from "Today" across weeks 1–3, then the plan-ready announcement. This is a more clinical/progress-oriented paywall lead-in vs the gamified discount.

## Ideas to Test for Fabulous Web Onboarding

### 🔴 High Priority

**1. Diagnostic question as entry hook**
Liven opens with "Is your nervous system dysregulated?" — a question the user doesn't know the answer to. Test replacing Fabulous's current benefit-led entry headline ("Heal Burnout, Build Habits") with a diagnostic question that creates immediate self-assessment curiosity, e.g. "Is your nervous system stuck in survival mode?" or "Are you burning out — or are you dysregulated?" This reframes onboarding as a test, not a sales funnel.

**2. Concrete outcome date before paywall**
Liven tells users: "Based on your answers, we expect you to decrease your nervous system dysregulation level by June 2026." Test adding a specific projected outcome date on Fabulous's paywall lead-in, e.g. "Based on your habits, you could form 3 new routines by [date in 6 weeks]." Specificity signals confidence and creates forward commitment.

**3. Progress chart as paywall lead-in (pre-price)**
The animated "Today → Week 2 → Week 3" trajectory chart appears just before pricing. Test showing users a personalised progress chart on Fabulous — where they are today vs where they'll be in 4 weeks — immediately before the pricing screen. Visualises the value of the subscription as a trajectory, not a product.

**4. Loading screen with high-quality testimonials**
Liven uses the "processing your plan" wait time to display Trustpilot reviews (⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ "Finally understood I wasn't 'lazy'" — Sarah J.). The testimonials are emotionally resonant and directly address the product's core insight. Test replacing Fabulous's generic loading animation with cycling testimonials that match the user's stated problem.

### 🟡 Medium Priority

**5. Emoji Likert scale for diagnostic questions**
The 5-emoji scale (❌ strongly disagree → 👍✨ strongly agree) is fast, emotionally intuitive, and keeps users moving. Test using emoji-scale screens for Fabulous's self-assessment section instead of text-only options — reduces decision weight and increases velocity.

**6. "Only X minutes" badge on entry**
Liven shows "Take 3-minute quiz" badge on the entry screen (even though it's longer). Test adding a time expectation badge to Fabulous's onboarding entry: "3-minute assessment" or "5 questions to personalise your plan." Reduces initial friction.

**7. Plan timeline visual (months with app previews)**
The April → June calendar showing app content thumbnails previews the product while framing results as a journey. Test adding a 4–8 week visual plan timeline on Fabulous pre-paywall that shows what content/habits they'll build, with specific month labels.

**8. "Did you hear about us from a therapist?" screen**
Liven asks this as their final quiz question — a subtle signal that therapists recommend the product. Test a similar referral source question late in Fabulous's quiz to imply clinical endorsement without making an explicit claim.

### 🟢 Lower Priority

**9. Childhood stress question for emotional deepening**
"Did you experience ongoing stress or emotional distance in childhood?" (Yes/Sometimes/No/Hard to say) is a high-stakes question that deepens emotional investment and implies Liven understands root causes, not just symptoms. Worth testing for Fabulous if ever introducing a trauma-informed or root-cause framing angle.

**10. Asymmetric email opt-in framing**
Liven's post-email opt-in uses: "Sure, I'm in!" (primary green button) vs faded grey text "I don't want to receive tips or updates." The opt-out is deliberately de-emphasised. Fabulous may already do this — but worth auditing the copy framing on the marketing opt-in screen.

**11. Remove homepage for paid traffic**
This URL variant lands directly at the quiz with no brand homepage. For paid traffic channels (AppLovin, Facebook), test removing the Fabulous homepage from the funnel entirely — start directly at gender/goal selection. Reduces one exit point.
