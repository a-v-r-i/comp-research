## Onboarding Flow Summary

### Stage 0 — Age Selection (screen 1)
No landing page copy — the page IS the first quiz step. A 2×2 grid of illustrated avatar cards (18-24, 25-34, 35-44, 45+) with no text prompt other than "Become the most interesting person in the room / 3-MINUTE QUIZ". Clicking a card navigates immediately to Q1.

### Stage 1 — Social Proof Burst (screen 2)
Before a single quiz question: "55+ million people already use the Headway app" + a Daily Mail quote + 6 press logos (Yahoo News, Forbes, TechCrunch, Entrepreneur, MakeUseOf, HackerNoon). This is a pre-qualification trust signal delivered at maximum commitment cost (zero — the user just tapped an age card).

### Stage 2 — PROFILE Quiz (~screens 3–17, ~15 questions)
Standard single-select questions with emoji + text options. Progress bar has 4 labeled stages: PROFILE, PATTERNS, (unlabeled), GOALS. Notable questions include:
- "Whose life principles, success, and personality inspire you most?" — celebrity grid (Branson, LeBron, Oprah, Emma Watson, Serena Williams, Jeff Bezos, Kevin Hart, Brené Brown)
- "Which describes you best?" — Leader / Follower / A bit of both
- Identity and aspiration questions with minimal friction

### Stage 3 — PATTERNS Quiz (~screens 18–27, ~10 questions)
Behavioral and mindset questions. Binary choices ("When you succeed, you'd rather… Think of what could be done better / Celebrate the win"). These build a psychological profile for personalization messaging.

### Stage 4 — Book Preference Cards (~screens 28–35, ~8 books)
Shows actual book covers one at a time with Yes/No buttons. The 80/20 Principle shown as one example. This primes users to visualise themselves reading books and creates product desire before the paywall.

### Stage 5 — Loading / Plan Creation (~screens 36–40)
"We are crafting your learning experience…" with a checklist (Goals ✓, Growth areas ✓, Content…). During loading, two modal interruptions appear:
1. "Do you want to give up when things get hard?" — No / Yes
2. "Are you inclined to finish what you start?" — No / Yes
Below the loading animation: live user testimonials ("It helps with many, from relationships to financial goals…"). These modals are commitment anchors — answering "No / Yes" triggers consistency bias.

### Stage 6 — Plan Ready (screen 41)
"Your personal growth plan is ready." Card shows a growth curve from "Now" (Mar 27) to 15 books/month (Apr 27). Includes "To use the app, you need an iPhone or Android smartphone" — early friction management.

### Stage 7 — Email Gate (screen 42)
Clean email form with two friction checkboxes (newsletter + T&C). Continue is disabled until both are checked. Below the form: three award badge images (SoftTech Best Mobile App Solution, Editor's Choice, App Of The Day). No Google/Apple SSO offered.

### Stage 8 — Pricing (screen 50)
"Choose your plan" with a countdown timer ("60% discount reserved for: 10:00"). Three tiers shown as per-day price with strikethrough:
- 1 month: ~~€0.91~~ €0.45/day → €13.41 total (Save 51%)
- **3 months (MOST POPULAR):** ~~€0.56~~ €0.22/day → €19.99 total (Save 60%)
- 12 months: ~~€0.30~~ €0.15/day → €52.99 total (Save 51%)

### Stage 9 — Checkout (screen 52)
Modal with "91% of users stay with us after the initial 30-day subscription." Payment options: PayPal, Google Pay, credit card. Trust badges: Verified Visa, Mastercard, McAfee, Norton. Total shown: €19.99 EUR.

**Estimated completion time:** ~8–12 minutes for a real user.

## Psychological Mechanisms Summary

| Mechanism | Where Used |
|-----------|-----------|
| Identity-first entry | Age avatar cards (not a text form) — you pick a persona, not data |
| Social proof before quiz | 55M+ users + press logos on screen 2, before any commitment |
| Aspirational role models | Celebrity inspiration grid — associates the product with famous achievers |
| Labeled progress stages | PROFILE → PATTERNS → … → GOALS — creates a journey feeling, not a form |
| Product taste-testing | Book interest cards show real titles — creates desire before paywall |
| Commitment anchoring (loading) | Modal yes/no questions during plan build ("Are you inclined to finish what you start?") exploit Cialdini's consistency principle |
| Social proof during loading | User testimonials scroll below the loading bar — fills dead time with trust |
| Outcome visualization | "15 books/month by April 27" growth chart — makes the result concrete and dated |
| Scarcity + urgency | 10:00 countdown timer on pricing page with "60% discount reserved" |
| Daily price framing | Shows €0.22/day rather than €19.99 — makes the price feel trivial |
| Retention claim at checkout | "91% of users stay" — reduces cancellation anxiety right at the payment step |
| Trust layering | Award badges at email gate; payment badges at checkout; both timed to peak anxiety moments |

## Ideas to Test for Fabulous Web Onboarding

1. **Age avatar card entry** — replace the current text-based first question with illustrated persona cards. Tapping a picture feels lower-commitment than selecting text; avatar identity primes self-concept from the first touch.

2. **Social proof burst before question 1** — insert a standalone screen showing "X million people use Fabulous" + 2-3 press logos immediately after the first tap, before the quiz starts. Headway does this after age selection; it validates the choice before asking anything hard.

3. **"Who inspires you?" question** — aspirational role model selection (e.g. athletes, CEOs, mindfulness leaders) maps user identity to product content and increases emotional investment in the quiz.

4. **Content taste-testing cards** — show Fabulous challenge/program covers (like book covers) mid-quiz with a Yes/No swipe. Builds desire for specific content before the paywall and creates a "personalized plan" feel.

5. **Commitment questions during loading** — add 1-2 modal yes/no questions during the plan-creation loading screen ("Are you committed to building a daily routine? No / Yes"). The act of answering Yes strengthens follow-through intent.

6. **Countdown timer on pricing** — "X% discount reserved for: 10:00" timer. Headway does this for 60% off; Fabulous could frame it as a welcome discount that expires. One of the highest-ROI pricing page tactics.

7. **Daily price anchoring** — surface per-day cost prominently (e.g., "Less than a coffee — €0.22/day") rather than leading with the monthly total.

8. **"91% of users stay" at checkout** — a specific retention statistic placed at the payment step directly addresses the biggest objection (fear of being charged after trial). Worth testing a Fabulous-specific number here.

9. **Two-checkbox email gate** — requiring explicit T&C + newsletter opt-in (rather than pre-checked) may reduce spam complaints and improve deliverability, even if it slightly lowers submission rate. Worth testing vs. single-checkbox.
