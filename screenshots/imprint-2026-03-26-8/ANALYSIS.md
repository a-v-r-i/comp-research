## Onboarding Flow Summary

**Estimated completion time:** 4–6 minutes
**Quiz screens:** ~32 questions + ~8 interstitials/loading screens
**Email gate position:** Screen 41 (after ~38 quiz screens)

### Stage 1 — Hook (1 screen)
"Replace scrolling with Buddhism." A clean landing with a Buddha illustration and "Take our 2-minute quiz to get started." The hook is anti-phone-addiction framing — Imprint positions itself as the replacement for the scroll habit, not just another app competing for time. The "2-minute" promise minimises commitment friction.

### Stage 2 — Demographics (2 screens)
Age selection only. Quick, non-intrusive.

### Stage 3 — Early social proof (1 screen)
"Over 5 million people are already learning Buddhism with us." Credibility injection before the user has invested any quiz time — reduces early drop-off.

### Stage 4 — Goals & interest discovery (5 screens)
Multi-select: why interested in Buddhism, which aspects intrigue you. Options include "Replace social media" — directly echoing the landing hook. Followed immediately by a warm interstitial: "We're here to help you achieve your goals!" with a Learn → Remember → Apply diagram.

### Stage 5 — Topic swiping (3 screens)
Auto-advancing Yes/No cards for individual Buddhist concepts (Impermanence, The Eightfold Path, etc.), each with a custom illustration. No Continue button — instant tap and advance. This is the highest-momentum section of the quiz; the rapid auto-advance creates an engagement loop similar to swiping.

### Stage 6 — Expert credibility interstitial (1 screen)
"Created by Buddhism experts" — trust signal before knowledge questions begin.

### Stage 7 — Knowledge self-assessment (2 screens)
"Are you satisfied with your knowledge of Buddhism?" — 👍 Yes / 🤚 A little / 👎 No. Auto-advance. Frames the quiz as a personalised diagnostic.

### Stage 8 — Skills & knowledge mini-quiz (5 screens)
Multi-select skills question, then three knowledge quiz questions ("Do you know what Buddhists mean by 'The Middle Way'?", "Are you familiar with what the Dhammapada teaches?", "Did you know that suffering is one of the Four Noble Truths?"). The knowledge questions double as micro-teaching moments — they inform even when you answer No. Followed by interstitial: "Here's something many people don't realize."

### Stage 9 — Learning behaviour (4 screens)
Time availability (Yes/Occasionally/No), biggest obstacles (multi-select), learning style (Big-picture ideas / Step-by-step details / Hands-on practice / Real-world examples / Stories and narratives). Product pitch interstitial: "Buddhism that you can put into practice — interactive, visual lessons designed to fit into your day."

### Stage 10 — Second knowledge round + motivation (5 screens)
Two more knowledge questions on Noble Eightfold Path and cause of suffering. "Backed by science. Designed to be fun and effective." credibility screens. Then "What's motivating you towards personal growth?" (Stay sharp / Advance my career / Become a more interesting person / Broaden my perspectives / Spend time productively).

### Stage 11 — Plan builder loading (4 screens)
"Crafting your learning experience..." — animated progress bar with sub-task labels (Setting goals, etc.) while a 5-star user testimonial displays below. This wait-screen makes the payoff feel earned and fills dead time with social proof.

### Stage 12 — Account creation gate (3 screens)
"Achieve your goals with Imprint — Create your free account." Email + password form. Below the form: Google App of the Year, Apple Editors' Choice, Apple Essential Education award badges. Account is created **before** any payment is requested — identity investment precedes the price.

### Stage 13 — Offer & paywall (3 screens)
1. **Risk reversal screen:** "Try Imprint, risk-free." — dedicated full screen for the 30-day money-back guarantee badge, shown before any pricing
2. **Plan selection:** 3 options (1 month / 3 months MOST POPULAR / 12 months) with auto-applied promo code SPRING2026 and a 9:59 countdown timer. Prices shown as per-day (€0.17/day for 3 months)
3. **Payment:** Stripe credit card form

| Mechanism | Where Used |
|-----------|------------|
| Anti-habit reframe | Landing: "Replace scrolling with Buddhism" |
| Low-friction promise | Landing: "2-minute quiz" |
| Early social proof (before investment) | Screen 4: "5 million people" |
| Micro-commitment via instant auto-advance | Topic swiping screens 8–10: no Continue button |
| Teaching as engagement | Knowledge quiz screens 16–18: educates on "No" answers |
| Interstitial validation loop | 5 interstitials confirm the user's choice throughout the quiz |
| Identity investment before price | Account creation (screen 41) precedes any plan/pricing |
| Award badges at email gate | Google/Apple awards on account creation form |
| Dedicated risk reversal screen | Full screen for 30-day guarantee before pricing |

## Ideas to Test for Fabulous Web Onboarding

1. **Anti-habit hook on landing** — "Replace doomscrolling with your morning ritual." Imprint's framing co-opts the phone-time behaviour instead of competing for it. Could be very relevant for Fabulous given its focus on habit-building.

2. **Auto-advance Yes/No topic cards** — A short "swipe through what matters to you" sequence (3–5 screens) before heavier multi-select questions. The instant advance with no Continue button creates strong momentum and reduces perceived quiz length.

3. **Knowledge questions as micro-teaching** — "Did you know...?" questions that educate on "No" answers. They make the quiz feel like learning has already started, which increases perceived value before the paywall.

4. **Dedicated guarantee screen before pricing** — Imprint gives the 30-day refund its own full screen before showing plan prices. Test separating the guarantee from the pricing UI to reduce anxiety right before the price reveal.

5. **Auto-applied promo code + countdown timer** — SPRING2026 code appears pre-filled with a timer on the pricing screen. The user feels they've already received a gift. Test an auto-applied code ("Your quiz discount") that expires within the session.

6. **Award badges on the email gate** — App of the Year / Editors' Choice logos placed directly on the email capture form. Test Fabulous's awards/press logos at the exact moment of email capture — the highest-friction conversion step.

7. **"Building your plan" loading screen with testimonial** — Imprint uses the plan-builder wait to show a 5-star review. Test injecting a testimonial on Fabulous's loading screens to fill dead time with social proof.

8. **Account creation before paywall** — Email collected and account created before showing pricing. Test moving Fabulous's email gate earlier in the funnel to increase identity investment before the price is shown.

9. **Learning style question → personalised plan copy** — "When learning I connect best with: Big-picture ideas / Step-by-step details / Hands-on practice…" — the answer could personalise the plan description or email subject line on the pricing page, making the plan feel bespoke.

10. **Per-day price framing** — Show the per-day equivalent prominently below the plan price on Fabulous's pricing screen. €0.17/day makes €15 feel trivial; similar framing on Fabulous's plans may reduce price sensitivity.
